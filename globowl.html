GloBowl

Overview

Fulfill the role of a UX and UI designer in producing a responsive recipe web app.

Objective

More and more people want to reduce their environmental impact. GloBowl aims to allows home cooks to see a recipe’s global footprint at a glance and make amendments to reduce that global footprint

My Process

Research

Challenge

What do users want?

Goal

I conducted interviews in order to find what users are looking for in a recipe web app, their frustrations, and what motivates interaction with one app over another. I wanted to find out whether they would use an app that gives guidance on ethical eating. Interviews were conducted via zoom for around 45 minutes.

Patterns
  • Using image quality & ratings to choose recipes
  • Frustrated by US measurements & long blogs
  • Desire to choose environmentally better foods
  • Desire to see the global impact of a recipe
  • Desire to have options when catering for people less inclined to choose alternatives

User Personas

Challenge

How to stay on track?

By highlighting patterns discovered during the user interviews, I built user personas, providing a focus for the design process.

Ideation

Problem

How to get users to engage with a new feature like a global footprint indicator, and the unfamiliar process of amending recipes to see the indicator change?.

Solution

The design of the indicator must be simple enough to be understood at a glance; mirroring a familiar feature, like a circular loading icon, and elements like dropdown menus for amending options, will aid usability.

User Flow

Challenge

What is the user's journey?

Following an MVP analysis where I established the primary user goals, I mapped out the user’s journey from entry point to goal achievement and began planning where design features would be required to get the user from A to B in the most efficient way.

Testing Early Prototyping

Challenge

Why are users struggling to navigate to the search bar?

Solution

When testing the basic wireframes I found that many users struggled to locate the search bar when included in the menu. I amended the design, ensuring that the search bar was immediately visible upon entry.

Challenge

How to interpret and apply feedback?

Solution

I received a compliment on the “feminine” aesthetic of the design, but I wanted the app to be more inclusive. I removed the illustrated borders and replaced them with thin, clean lines; I shifted any of the blush toned backgrounds (minimal as they were) to the grey tone in my palette. I definitely think this makes for a more inclusive and less fussy aesthetic. It was a real lesson in how sticking to the brief, user goal, and user personas can ensure that even feedback that is intended to be positive, when interpreted within context can ensure future iterations move in the right direction.

Wireframes

Challenge

What is the best layout for the recipe page?

Solution

The main recipe page is extremely important and so I put it under user preference testing to ensure I chose the best design; based on user feedback, the layout progressed to maximise space for content whilst making the most of high quality imagery.

Design

Mood Boards

Challenge

What theme is most suitable?

Solution

I wanted to use earth tones to reflect the environmental ethos. Greens evoke thoughts of fresh, healthy food as well as the planet and growth.

While I like both options, the tone of Mood Board A is more traditional for a recipe app. Users need to be engaged before they will interact with the unfamiliar global footprint design element, therefore it seemed most logical to stick with a more welcoming and recognisable aesthetic.

Style Guide

Challenge

How to keep the design consistent?

Solution

Maintaining integrity is paramount when building user trust and encouranging engagement with the Global Footprint feature; the style guide ensures that I, the design team, and any future iterations stay on brand.

Based on current recommendations, (or the user can set their own daily target) each recipe displays a global footprint animation (with a percentage) to show users, at a glance, how much of their daily footprint the recipe will use.

Conclusion

Achievement

I have created a responsive web app that allows users to see a recipe’s global footprint at a glance and make amendments to reduce that footprint. The solution to aid user engagement by mirroring a circular loading icon for the global footprint indicator and using dropdown menus as part of the ingredient swap feature worked well. Final iterations increased user understanding by placing the percentage (of the user’s daily food footprint with the current selection of ingredients) was moved from beneath the indicator to sit inside it instead; this move better incorporated it into the design element and made it easier to understand at a glance.

Changes

What changed was the my view of the user. The fact that every user said that they either didn’t eat meat or were trying to eat less meat but cooked for others who wanted to eat meat made me realise that there were very few apps that gave non-vegetarians / non-vegans, options and incentives to choose foods better for the environment. This meant that I began creating an app aimed at home cooks of all diets but that provided alternative options, making the design element that allowed users to swap out ingredients a key factor

Next Time

Users were very interested in knowing where their ingredients come from. A future iteration may include links in the ingredients list to suggested brands that align with GloBowl values of ethically sourced, local ingredients.
