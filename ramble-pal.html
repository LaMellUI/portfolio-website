Ramble Pal

Overview

Design both iOS and Android versions of a hiking app; aiding trail walkers to find, filter and navigate routes. Allowing users to choose between various views of trail information, access up-to-date trail warnings from other users, and see at a glance where they are in relation to the trail as a whole.

Objective

With an increasing appreciation of what the outdoors can do for physical and mental health, people are looking for new trails to discover, both locally and further afield. There is room for an app that supports users of all levels during their hike in a simple way that requires minimal user effort and maximum enjoyment of the outdoors.

My Process

Research

Problem 1

It is not currently possible to check-off waymarkers en route so that the user's current location within a trail, and the next instructions, can be seen at a glance, causing frustration and wasting time.

Goal

Allow users access to Waymarker, Map, and AR views to see at a glance their location within a trail and all pertinant information for the next stages.

Problem 2

The world is constantly changing and crucial trail information may be out of date, or season dependent. Users want to enjoy their time outdoors rather than spend that time solving unexpected problems.

Goal

Allow users to share trail updates specific to applicable points of the trail.

Competitor Analysis

Inspiration

  • Displaying the number of trails available in the user's country or defined area, lets users know if filtering is necessary
  • A simple navigation bar with few top-level page destinations is very user friendly
  • Attaching the live navigation button to the top/bottom of the screen as a FAB allows immediate access from anywhere within the app
  • Vibrant greens are fun and evoke images of nature and the environment
  • An animated location pin allows users to differentiate it from other map info and quickly see their location

Improvement

  • An excessive number of stats is difficult to take in. Show only the most necessary; users may adjust what they see in their settings
  • Long descriptive passages make it difficult to find the most useful information
  • Images are engaging but appropriate valuable space on a small device
  • Icons must be easy to tell apart and clearly signify their meaning

Ideation

User Flow

The secondary goal requires the user to register and set up a profile to comment and review, so this is the entry point of the app. However, as some users may not wish to register or comment there is the option to skip this step. I then prioritised the primary goal of viewing waymarker, map, and AR info via searching trails and filtering results.

Gestures

All gestures are as defined by the Human Interface and Material Design guidelines. All maps are fully interactive with double tap or pinch and spread for zoom as well as drag and rotate.

Sounds and Haptics

Conventional notification and error sounds as per the Human Interface and Material Design Guidelines.

Waymarker reached: → Strong alert sound and vibration (due to outdoor noise)

Waymarker has user comments: → Message ping and vibrations

Users may turn one or both of these off in the settings, and also request that they only be notified of messages from within a recent timeframe.

Wireframes

Using the current human interface and material design guidelines I designed an iOS and Android version of the app. Elements like the buttons, navigation bar, and tab navigation will be familar to users of the applicable native design. User Testing

Positive
  • Eye catching logo, use of colour and details
  • Clean and inviting design
  • Info is easy to see at a glance
  • Good layout and logical hierarchy
  • Easy to navigate
Critique
  • iOS nav bar icons are larger than other app icons
  • Live Waymarker page looks a little unfinished, inconsistent with the rest of the app
  • Clarity as to which pin pop-up is attached to on map view
  • Info of time and distance on live mode would be useful
  • Easy accessible start button but perhaps it being larger or a different colour would make it easier to spot
Solution
  • Adjust nav bar icons to the same size as the other icons
  • Create a collapsable list instead of info cards
  • Mute map with overlay and ensure the pop-up lies just beneath the relevant pin
  • Use gradient overlay theme to add this info to live screens
  • Use an extended floating action button (as per Material Design guidelines) and centre

Conclusion

What Went Well

Protyping and user testing. I find the process of protyping is a great way to discover where elements have been viewed from a different persepctive and may need some tweaking.

Research into existing apps was a fantastic springboard for applying key design elements and highlighting problem areas for which I might want to find a solution.

Challenges

Navigating the iOS and Material Design guidelines. I wanted to utilise the guidelines as much as possible but keep the app's design cohesive across the two platforms. I'll look into incorporating the guidelines into my regular skills progression.

I wanted to use a largely monochrome colour scheme and it took me a few iterations before I found something I was happy with but I think the speed at which I came to a decision and applied it to my wireframes has improved.

Improvement

I would like to introduce a Country Code design element for those people less used using countryside trails; I'd like to integrate it into the trail info and live trail pages e.g. at a gate, a tappable icon would remind people to leave the gate how they found it.

In terms of my own skills; I would love to improve my ability to create illustration as I think this will be an asset moving forwards, particularly with onboarding features.
